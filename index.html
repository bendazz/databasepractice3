Question 1 short answer

Given the two 3-dimensional vectors

$$\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix},$$

compute the dot product $\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b}$.

Answer

$\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{b} = 0$.

Reasoning. The dot product of two vectors of the same length is the sum of the products of corresponding entries.

  • $2 \cdot 4 = 8$
  • $(-1) \cdot 2 = -2$
  • $3 \cdot (-2) = -6$
  • Sum: $8 + (-2) + (-6) = 0$.

A dot product of zero means the vectors are orthogonal (perpendicular).

Question 2 short answer

Given the 3-dimensional vector

$$\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 4 \\ 8 \end{bmatrix},$$

compute its magnitude $\|\mathbf{v}\|$.

Answer

$\|\mathbf{v}\| = 9$.

Reasoning. The magnitude (Euclidean / L2 norm) of a vector is the square root of the sum of the squares of its entries.

  • $1^2 = 1$
  • $4^2 = 16$
  • $8^2 = 64$
  • Sum: $1 + 16 + 64 = 81$.
  • $\sqrt{81} = 9$.
Question 3 short answer

Given the two 3-dimensional vectors

$$\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix},$$

compute their cosine similarity.

Answer

$\cos\theta = \dfrac{8}{9}$.

Reasoning. Compute the dot product and each magnitude, then divide.

  • Dot product: $1\cdot 2 + 2\cdot 1 + 2\cdot 2 = 2 + 2 + 4 = 8$.
  • $\|\mathbf{a}\| = \sqrt{1^2 + 2^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{9} = 3$.
  • $\|\mathbf{b}\| = \sqrt{2^2 + 1^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{9} = 3$.
  • $\cos\theta = \dfrac{8}{3 \cdot 3} = \dfrac{8}{9}$.

A value close to 1 means the two vectors point in nearly the same direction.

Question 4 multiple choice

Consider the two 4-dimensional vectors

$$\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \\ 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}.$$

Which statement best describes the relationship between $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$?

  1. They are perpendicular.
  2. They point in the same direction.
  3. They point in opposite directions.
  4. None of the above.
Answer

A. They are perpendicular.

Reasoning. Compute the dot product:

  • $1 \cdot 2 = 2$
  • $2 \cdot (-1) = -2$
  • $(-1) \cdot 1 = -1$
  • $1 \cdot 1 = 1$
  • Sum: $2 + (-2) + (-1) + 1 = 0$.

A dot product of $0$ means the vectors are perpendicular (orthogonal). Vectors pointing in the same direction would have a positive dot product equal to $\|\mathbf{a}\|\,\|\mathbf{b}\|$, and opposite directions would give the negative of that product.

Question 5 multiple choice

Consider the two 4-dimensional vectors

$$\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \\ 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -6 \\ 9 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix}.$$

Which statement best describes the relationship between $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$?

  1. They are perpendicular.
  2. They point in the same direction.
  3. They point in opposite directions.
  4. None of the above.
Answer

B. They point in the same direction.

Reasoning. Notice that each entry of $\mathbf{b}$ is exactly $3$ times the corresponding entry of $\mathbf{a}$:

  • $3 = 3 \cdot 1$, $\;-6 = 3 \cdot (-2)$, $\;9 = 3 \cdot 3$, $\;-3 = 3 \cdot (-1)$.
  • So $\mathbf{b} = 3\mathbf{a}$, a positive scalar multiple of $\mathbf{a}$.

When one vector is a positive scalar multiple of the other, they point in the same direction (cosine similarity $= 1$). A negative scalar would mean opposite directions, and a dot product of $0$ would mean perpendicular.

Question 6 multiple choice

Consider the two 4-dimensional vectors

$$\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ -3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ -2 \\ 6 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}.$$

Which statement best describes the relationship between $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$?

  1. They are perpendicular.
  2. They point in the same direction.
  3. They point in opposite directions.
  4. None of the above.
Answer

C. They point in opposite directions.

Reasoning. Each entry of $\mathbf{b}$ is exactly $-2$ times the corresponding entry of $\mathbf{a}$:

  • $-4 = -2 \cdot 2$, $\;-2 = -2 \cdot 1$, $\;6 = -2 \cdot (-3)$, $\;-2 = -2 \cdot 1$.
  • So $\mathbf{b} = -2\mathbf{a}$, a negative scalar multiple of $\mathbf{a}$.

When one vector is a negative scalar multiple of the other, they point in opposite directions (cosine similarity $= -1$).

Question 7 multiple choice

Consider the two 4-dimensional vectors

$$\mathbf{a} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 1 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \mathbf{b} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ 3 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}.$$

Which statement best describes the relationship between $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$?

  1. They are perpendicular.
  2. They point in the same direction.
  3. They point in opposite directions.
  4. None of the above.
Answer

D. None of the above.

Reasoning. Check each possibility:

  • Dot product: $1\cdot 2 + 2\cdot 1 + 1\cdot 3 + (-1)\cdot 1 = 2 + 2 + 3 - 1 = 6$. Since this is not $0$, the vectors are not perpendicular.
  • For same or opposite direction, $\mathbf{b}$ would need to be a scalar multiple of $\mathbf{a}$. The entry ratios $b_i / a_i$ are $2,\ \tfrac{1}{2},\ 3,\ -1$ — not all equal — so $\mathbf{b}$ is not a scalar multiple of $\mathbf{a}$.

The vectors are neither perpendicular nor parallel, so the correct choice is none of the above.

Question 8 multiple choice

A vector database stores three short documents along with their 2-dimensional embeddings:

  • Doc A: "Please take out the recycling bin."  →  $(-3,\ 4)$
  • Doc B: "Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun."  →  $(4,\ 3)$
  • Doc C: "She finished the novel in two days."  →  $(-4,\ -3)$

A user searches with the query

Query: "The garden needs watering this afternoon."  →  $\mathbf{q} = (3,\ 4)$.

Which document should the vector database return as the most similar match?

  1. Doc A
  2. Doc B
  3. Doc C
Answer

B. Doc B.

Reasoning. Every vector here has magnitude $\sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5$, so each cosine similarity is the dot product divided by $5 \cdot 5 = 25$.

  • $\mathbf{q} \cdot \text{A} = 3\cdot(-3) + 4\cdot 4 = 7 \;\Rightarrow\; \cos\theta = \tfrac{7}{25}$
  • $\mathbf{q} \cdot \text{B} = 3\cdot 4 + 4\cdot 3 = 24 \;\Rightarrow\; \cos\theta = \tfrac{24}{25}$
  • $\mathbf{q} \cdot \text{C} = 3\cdot(-4) + 4\cdot(-3) = -24 \;\Rightarrow\; \cos\theta = -\tfrac{24}{25}$

Doc B has the highest cosine similarity, so it is the nearest neighbor the vector database would return. The sentences themselves are semantically unrelated — the decision comes purely from the geometry of the embeddings.
